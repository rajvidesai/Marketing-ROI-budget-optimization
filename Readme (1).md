# Marketing ROI & Budget Reallocation (Attribution + Regression)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes marketing performance across multiple channels to identify which campaigns generate the highest return on investment. 

Using attribution modeling, regression analysis, and an automated ETL pipeline, the project evaluates marketing efficiency and proposes a data-driven budget reallocation strategy designed to maximize revenue without increasing the total marketing budget.

The analysis is supported by a Power BI dashboard that provides interactive insights into channel performance, campaign efficiency, and customer segments.

The core challenge addressed is the "messy" reality of marketing data—missing values, inconsistent casing, and complex customer journeys. By building a robust ETL pipeline and applying both heuristic (Attribution) and probabilistic (Regression) models, we provide a decision-grade answer to:
1. Which channels/campaigns are actually driving revenue?
2. How should the next month's budget be reallocated to maximize ROI?
3. What is the confidence level and sensitivity of these recommendations?

## Datasets Provided
The analysis is powered by a multi-dimensional dataset located in the `raw_data/` folder:
- **`users.csv`**: Profiles, signup dates, and demographic segments.
- **`campaigns.csv`**: Metadata including channel, objective, and campaign timelines.
- **`ad_spend_daily.csv`**: Daily spend, impressions, and clicks at the campaign level.
- **`sessions.csv`**: Granular clickstream metadata (channel, campaign, device).
- **`orders.csv`**: Transactional records linked to sessions.
- **`order_items.csv`**: Basket composition and quantities.
- **`products.json`**: Product catalog (category, price, cost) used for margin proxy.

## Part A — Business Framing & KPI Definition

### 1. Business Objective
The objective of this project is to evaluate the effectiveness of marketing spend across multiple channels and campaigns and to recommend an optimized budget allocation strategy that maximizes incremental revenue under a fixed monthly marketing budget constraint.

The analysis aims to move beyond simple historical reporting and provide a forward-looking, decision-grade framework for capital allocation.

---

### 2. Problem Statement
The company invests across multiple marketing channels including Search, Paid Social, Email, Referral, and Organic. However, it currently lacks a structured, data-driven framework to measure the incremental revenue contribution of each channel and campaign.

Without a clear attribution model and impact estimation framework, budget allocation decisions may lead to inefficient spending, over-investment in low-impact channels, and under-investment in high-performing channels.

This project seeks to design a reproducible analytics pipeline that:
1. Attributes revenue to specific marketing touchpoints.
2. Estimates the marginal impact of marketing spend while controlling for seasonality and trends.
3. Recommends a data-driven reallocation of next month’s marketing budget.

---

### 3. North Star Metric
**Incremental Revenue per ₹ Spent**

The North Star metric for this project is Incremental Revenue per ₹ Spent. Unlike traditional ROAS, which measures historical average revenue divided by total spend, this estimates the marginal revenue generated by an additional unit of marketing investment after controlling for seasonality, trend effects, and other observable factors.

---

### 4. Supporting KPIs
| Metric Type | KPI | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Volume** | Total Attributed Revenue | Revenue assigned to channels via Last-Touch. |
| **Efficiency** | ROAS | Attributed Revenue / Marketing Spend. |
| **Efficiency** | CAC (Proxy) | Marketing Spend / Total Orders. |
| **Funnel** | CVR (%) | Orders / Sessions. |
| **Monetization** | AOV | Total Revenue / Total Orders. |
| **Profitability** | Margin Proxy | Revenue - Product Cost. |

---

### 5. Attribution Rule
**Last-Touch Attribution**: Each completed order is attributed to the channel and campaign associated with the final session preceding the purchase. This method ensures consistent and transparent revenue allocation across channels for bottom-funnel performance.

---

### 6. Stakeholder Questions
1. Which campaigns are wasting spend with high CAC and low revenue?
2. How does performance differ for New vs. Returning users?
3. What is the side-by-side comparison of attributed ROAS vs. blended ROAS?
4. What is the estimated revenue lift if we shift 20% of the budget from Social to Email?
5. How does seasonality (weekday vs. weekend) affect conversion?

## Part B — Data Architecture & ETL (Python)

### 1. Pipeline Architecture
The ETL pipeline handles raw logs from `raw_data/` and produces curated fact tables in `data/`.
- **`fact_sessions`**: 1 row per session; includes `user_id`, `channel`, `is_new_user`, and `purchase_flag`.

## Part D — Attribution Analysis

### 1. Methodology
We implemented **Last-Touch Attribution (LTA)** using user session history. Each order's `net_revenue` is mapped back to the unique `channel` that generated the final session within the conversion window.

### 2. Attributed vs. Blended ROAS
| Channel | Attributed ROAS | Blended ROAS | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Paid Social** | 0.64 | 3.63 | High Assistance - Low Conversion |
| **Search** | 1.29 | 3.04 | Core Revenue Driver |
| **Email** | 4.99 | 26.64 | High-Intent Closer |

**Interpretation**: Paid Social acts as a "Discovery" channel. It seeds the top of the funnel (Blended ROAS is high) but is rarely the "closer" (Attributed ROAS is low). Cutting Social entirely could starve bottom-funnel channels of high-intent traffic.

## Part E — Regression Impact Modeling

### 1. Model Objective
To estimate the **Marginal Effect** of spend by channel on Daily Revenue, controlling for seasonality (`day_of_week`) and promotions (`promo_flag`).

### 2. Model Performance
- **Algorithm**: Linear Regression (Log-Log transformation to capture diminishing returns).
- **Metric**: 
    - **R-Squared**: 0.419
    - **RMSE**: 86,556.05
    - **MAE**: 67,670.65
- **Findings**:
    - **Search Coefficient**: 1.12 (For every 1% increase in spend, revenue increases by 1.12%).
    - **Paid Social Coefficient**: 0.34 (Low incremental impact, high saturation).
    - **Email Coefficient**: 1.58 (Highest efficiency per incremental dollar).
